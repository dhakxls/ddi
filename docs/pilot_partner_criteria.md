# Pilot Partner Identification Criteria

## Target Partner Profile

### Ideal Partner Characteristics

**Organization Type**
- Pharmaceutical companies (small to mid-size preferred)
- Clinical Research Organizations (CROs)
- Academic medical centers with clinical trial programs
- Biotechnology companies

**Therapeutic Areas of Interest**
- Cardiovascular (anticoagulants, antiarrhythmics)
- Central Nervous System (psychiatry, neurology)
- Oncology
- Infectious disease
- Metabolic disorders (diabetes)

**Development Stage**
- Phase 2 or Phase 3 clinical trials
- Multiple compounds in development pipeline
- Compounds with known or suspected DDI potential

**Technical Capability**
- In-house clinical pharmacology team
- Access to DDI study data
- Willingness to share anonymized data for validation
- Interest in improving DDI prediction accuracy

## Partner Value Proposition

### For Partners
- **Risk Reduction**: Early identification of potential DDI issues before costly clinical trials
- **Cost Savings**: Reduced need for extensive DDI studies through better prediction
- **Time Savings**: Faster go/no-go decisions on compound combinations
- **Regulatory Advantage**: Proactive DDI assessment aligns with FDA/EMA expectations
- **Data Insights**: Access to aggregated DDI patterns across therapeutic areas

### For Us
- **Real-world Validation**: Test predictions against actual clinical data
- **Feedback Loop**: Improve models based on pilot outcomes
- **Case Studies**: Build credibility for broader commercialization
- **Data Access**: Anonymized data for model refinement (with consent)

## Outreach Strategy

### Initial Contact (Q3 2026)
1. **Identify Targets**
   - Scan clinical trial registries (ClinicalTrials.gov)
   - Review conference abstracts (ASCPT, AAPS, DIA)
   - Leverage existing network and academic collaborations
   - Target 20-30 potential partners

2. **Outreach Materials**
   - One-page executive summary
   - Technical white paper on methodology
   - Case study examples (if available from initial data)
   - Pilot engagement proposal (timeline, deliverables, data requirements)

3. **Engagement Channels**
   - Direct outreach to clinical pharmacology leaders
   - Conference presentations and networking
   - Academic collaborations and referrals
   - Industry associations (DIA, ASCPT)

### Pilot Selection Criteria (Q4 2026)
- **Data Availability**: Access to historical DDI study data
- **Therapeutic Fit**: Compounds in our initial 100-compound dataset
- **Commitment Level**: Willingness to dedicate resources to pilot
- **Timeline Alignment**: Ability to complete pilot within 6 months
- **Geographic Consideration**: Prefer US/EU for regulatory alignment

## Pilot Structure

### Pilot Duration: 6 months

### Phase 1: Data Integration (Month 1-2)
- Partner provides compound data (enzyme profiles, known DDIs)
- We curate and integrate into our system
- Identify gaps and additional data needs

### Phase 2: Prediction Testing (Month 3-4)
- Run predictions on partner's compounds
- Compare predictions to known DDI data
- Identify high-risk compound combinations

### Phase 3: Validation Planning (Month 5)
- Design validation experiments for high-risk predictions
- Partner conducts or plans validation studies
- Document outcomes and discrepancies

### Phase 4: Analysis and Feedback (Month 6)
- Analyze prediction accuracy
- Identify model improvement opportunities
- Prepare joint case study (with partner approval)
- Discuss expanded collaboration

### Deliverables
- DDI risk assessment report for partner's compounds
- Validation experiment recommendations
- Prediction accuracy metrics
- Joint case study (optional)
- Feedback report on model performance

## Success Metrics

### Quantitative
- Prediction accuracy (>70% for major DDI categories)
- Reduction in unnecessary DDI studies (>30%)
- Time savings in DDI assessment (>50%)
- Partner satisfaction score (>4/5)

### Qualitative
- Partner willingness to continue collaboration
- Regulatory acceptance of predictions
- Publication opportunities
- Referrals to additional partners

## Risk Mitigation

- **Data Access**: Offer data use agreements and clear IP terms
- **Validation Costs**: Share validation study costs or use existing data
- **Timeline**: Build flexibility into pilot schedule
- **Competition**: Emphasize unique methodology and transparency
- **Regulatory Acceptance**: Engage regulatory consultants early
